# Case Study: UPI Payment Fraud

UPI payment fraud is a common cybercrime in India where attackers trick users into sharing sensitive information or approving fraudulent transactions. In a typical case, a scammer contacts the victim pretending to be from a bank or service provider. They send a fake payment request or a malicious link.

The victim is asked to enter their UPI PIN or click on the link, which leads to unauthorized transactions. Often, people believe they are receiving money but instead end up approving a payment request. This results in financial loss.

Students and elderly people are usually targeted as they may not be fully aware of such scams. The consequences include loss of money, stress, and misuse of personal information.

This type of fraud highlights the importance of digital literacy and awareness. Users must understand that UPI PIN should never be shared and should only be used when sending money, not receiving it.
